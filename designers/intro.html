Intro: Photoshop != CSS

Photoshop is an incredible tool for web design and offers a robust suite of tools to generate digital graphics for use in web production. Because of it's versatility, ease of use, and reliability, it's become the industry standard for creating webpages.

However, Photoshop does not generate CSS. Photoshop was built as and continues to be a tool primarily for munipulating digital photography. A PSD in it's current form can not output CSS and HTML natively. Fireworks can, but Fireworks sucks at doing it.

Because of Photoshops inability to speak a native web langauge, developers spend a lot of time translating PSDs into webpages. It's a time consuming process know as "front-end development" and some of us make a damn fine living doing it.

Once a project is mirrored from a PSD into CSS a lot of times it can feel like a "spot the difference" from Highlights magazine.

One little tweak grows out of control, and the PSD has to be mirrored on mobile, tablet, and across browsers. One thing leads to another and suddenly what began as a minor touch up job becomes this:
